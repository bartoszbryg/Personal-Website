About Me

Hi, I’m Bartosz Bryg – a third-year Computer Science student at NJIT, minoring in Drones and Robotics and maintaining a 4.0 GPA. I’m part of the Albert Dorman Honors College and an NCAA Division I swimmer, which has sharpened my discipline, time management, and commitment to excellence.

At NJIT’s Institute for Data Science, I work on high-performance graph algorithms for large-scale network analysis, collaborating with PhD researchers and faculty. Our projects, including partnerships with Harvard and the University of Illinois, support breakthroughs in brain network and AI research. I was recently recognized with the Grace Hopper AI Research Award for my work on cluster analysis.

My background bridges software and hardware - from distributed graph analytics to building intelligent robotic systems. I’m passionate about developing efficient, real-world tech that advances both research and society.
